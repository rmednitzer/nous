# Audit 2026-06-15b: documentation and hardware-profile cross-check

A focused adversarial pass at HEAD (`471c029`, after PR #160), on the three
fronts requested directly: every code comment, every markdown document, and the
hardware profile numbers cross-checked against trusted external sources (vendor
datasheets, NIST/CRC reference data, and standards bodies). Three read-only
agents swept the comments, the markdown, and the profiles in parallel; each
finding below was re-validated by hand against the code or the cited source
before any edit.

The headline results are a cluster of documentation that had drifted out of step
with shipped code (the DTN mesh and the daily audit anchor were both described as
absent or optional when both are live), a stale spelled-out tool count in three
live documents, and three genuine errors in the bill of materials, the worst of
which nearly doubled a battery capacity. All are fixed in this same change. A
handful of externally-ambiguous or stylistic items are recorded at the end
without a code change and explained.

## Findings: hardware profiles and BOM (fixed)

The profile numbers were checked against vendor datasheets and reference data.
Most of the bill of materials held up: the Jetson AGX Orin (15-60 W, Tj,max
105 C) and Orin NX (10-25 W) power envelopes, the Raspberry Pi 5 idle draw, the
PowerFilm SOL90 90 W panel, the methanol energy chain (density 0.79 g/ml, LHV
5.53 Wh/g, 0.9 L/kWh implying ~1.4 Wh/g and ~25% system efficiency), the
BB-2590/U voltage and chemistry, and every USB-PD profile all confirmed. The
errors below did not.

| Id | Sev | Item | Was | Now and source |
|----|-----|------|-----|----------------|
| P-1 | HIGH | pi5-hailo `battery_wh` | 99 Wh | 55 Wh. Samsung INR18650-25R (2500 mAh, 3.7 V): 6 cells x ~9.25 Wh = ~55 Wh. 99 Wh implies ~16.5 Wh/cell, impossible for a 2500 mAh cell. |
| P-2 | MED | BOM 18650 per-cell energy | ~16.5 Wh/cell | ~9.25 Wh/cell. 2.5 Ah x 3.7 V = 9.25 Wh. Root cause of P-1. |
| P-3 | MED | BB-2590/U capacity | 296 Wh | 294 Wh. Bren-Tronics BT-70791CG (9.9 Ah) is 294 Wh; the AGX profile already used 588 = 2 x 294, so 296 was internally inconsistent. orin-nx profile and BOM aligned to 294. |
| P-4 | MED | STANAG 4074 attribution | "NATO 28 V vehicle bus" | MIL-STD-1275. STANAG 4074 is the jump-start connector standard for 24 V starting; the 28 V DC tactical-vehicle bus is MIL-STD-1275. Fixed in both profile comments and the BOM. |
| P-5 | LOW | EFOY Pro 800 nameplate | 40 W | ~45 W (25 W minimum), per the SFC EFOY Pro 800 datasheet. The profile's 25 W continuous is within spec; only the BOM anchor was off. |
| P-6 | LOW | Hailo-8L power | "2-5 W" | ~1.5 W typical, up to ~6.6 W for the M.2 module. |
| P-7 | LOW | BB-2590/U weight | ~2.2 kg | ~1.4 kg. Bren-Tronics BT-70791 variants are 1.4-1.5 kg. BOM note only, not a profile field. |

## Recorded, not changed

- **Spot battery (605 Wh, 41.6 V) and CORE I/O (30/90 W).** Current vendor
  listings show ~564 Wh and a 35-58.8 V range with no stated nominal, and no
  public datasheet gives the CORE I/O watt figures. Substituting a reseller
  number for the manufacturer spec would trade one unsourced figure for another,
  so the demonstration profile keeps its values and the BOM now carries the
  caveat.
- **`anthropic_client.py` thinking-tier comment.** It names model tiers
  ("Sonnet 4.6 / Opus 4.6+") to document which gain thinking. That is domain
  documentation, not the session's configured model identifier, and the file is
  an ADR-gated surface; left as-is.
- **Section-divider comments** (`# -- foo ---`) in comms_outbox.py, dtn_mesh.py,
  and oauth.py, plus the plain section labels in config.py. They name a section
  rather than a rationale, but they are a deliberate navigation idiom local to
  the larger modules; stripping roughly twenty of them is churn against local
  consistency, so they stay.
- **`--` used as prose punctuation** in ADR 0018 and ADR 0041. The convention
  discourages it, but it is pervasive across the historical ADR corpus and these
  are accepted decision records; not selectively rewritten.

No finding reopens a 2026-06-14b or 2026-06-15 item.
