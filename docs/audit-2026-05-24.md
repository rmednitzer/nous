# AUDIT 2026-05-24

Full code-index audit of `nous` against documented standards and the
project's own invariants. This is a delta audit against the 2026-05-23
baseline ([`docs/audit-2026-05-23.md`](audit-2026-05-23.md), including
its §10 re-audit at HEAD `43d0db2`). It records which findings the
post-baseline merge train pinned in regression code, which new defensive
measures landed, which carry-forward findings remain open, and what the
fresh validation against the conformance documents and known good
sources surfaced.

Conducted: 2026-05-24.
Branch audited: `claude/vibrant-mendel-0J5JN` against `main`.
Source revision: `fb8356f` (Merge PR #44, "borrow regression suite +
tick finiteness guard + 4 ADRs from 6dof review").

## 1. Executive summary

The repository is in noticeably better shape than the 2026-05-23
baseline. The borrowed regression suite at
`tests/regression/test_audit_findings.py` formally pins the closed C1,
C4, C5, H3, and M8 findings: each carries the prior defect in its class
docstring and asserts the specific behaviour that closed it, so a future
change that re-opens any of them surfaces here rather than going
re-discovered from scratch. The matching tick-boundary finiteness guard
in `engine._assert_post_tick_finite` (`src/nous/engine.py:242-275`)
catches the C5-class "stub pretending to be a real filter" failure mode
at the source: any non-finite estimator point or non-finite / negative
covariance trips a `RuntimeError` at the end of the tick that produced
it, rather than poisoning the next tick. Four Proposed ADRs (0019
through 0022) capture the design work for the patterns that did not
fit a single PR and are tracked for L1 follow-through.

Quality gates are green: ruff clean, mypy strict clean across 58 source
files, **365 pytest tests pass** (up from 351 at the 2026-05-23 §10
re-audit; +14 cover the regression suite), `make policy` passes, and
the em-dash grep returns zero hits across the markdown tree.

The validation against the conformance documents in `docs/conformance/`
matches the code on every adapter that has shipped: CoT 2.0 required
attributes, MISB ST 0601 BER length handling, OGC SensorThings v1.1
§6.5 UTC normalisation, and NMEA 0183 v4.10 full GGA are all present
in the encoder paths and pinned by the regression suite. The
documented limitations (no mesh delivery on CoT, no full ST 0601 tag
dictionary, no streaming on SensorThings, no multi-talker prefixes on
NMEA) are explicit and unchanged.

The audit found no privilege-escalation, no remote-code-execution, no
audit-bypass classes of bug, and no new critical findings. Every
carry-forward open finding from the 2026-05-23 §10 re-audit was
re-verified directly in the source and remains open in the same shape.
The single substantive new observation (N8) is that the four
Proposed ADRs (0019 through 0022) have no implementation yet: their
target seams (the seed/clock thread, property-based subsystem
invariants, per-subsystem tool modules, the runtime safety enforcer)
are still in the design phase. The two new L1 subsystems that landed
since the 2026-05-20 baseline (sensors, biometrics) also have no entry
under `docs/conformance/`; this is consistent with the L9 baseline gap
on MQTT and is tracked under the same theme.

## 4. Validation against documented standards

This section walks each interop adapter and matches the implementation
against the conformance document that governs it. The conformance
documents are the project's own "known good" source: they enumerate
what is supported, what is omitted, and what would be required for a
certified posture.

### 4.1 CoT 2.0 (`src/nous/interop/cot.py`)

The `CotAdapter.encode()` writes the four required temporal /
provenance attributes (`time`, `start`, `stale`, `how="m-g"`) on every
event, plus the `type` (default `a-f-G-U-C`), `version`, and `uid`.
The point element carries lat, lon, hae, ce, and le. Attribute values
are escaped with `xml.sax.saxutils.quoteattr`. The encoder refuses to
emit when the source estimate is older than `max_age_s` (default 60 s,
SC-4). The decoder explicitly refuses `DOCTYPE` and `ENTITY`
declarations (XXE-safe). All of this matches `docs/conformance/cot-tak.md`.

The conformance document calls out the omissions: mesh delivery, COP
overlays, encrypted variants, the wider `detail` schema (track,
image, shape). None of these are misrepresented as supported by the
code; the encoder's surface is narrow and honest.

### 4.2 MISB ST 0601 (`src/nous/interop/misb_klv.py`)

The encoder validates keys to the `[1, 255]` range and refuses to
encode out-of-range keys; BER length uses short form below 128 bytes
and long form (1, 2, 4, or 8 length bytes) above. The encoder raises
`ValueError` rather than truncating either the key or the length. Key
2 (Unix Time Stamp) is stamped on every encode. The decoder validates
the universal key, parses the BER length, walks the TLV stream, and
returns `{"error": ...}` on any truncation or malformation. All of
this matches `docs/conformance/stanag-4609-misb-klv.md`.

The conformance document is clear that the universal-set checksum
(key 1) is omitted, that the full ST 0601 tag dictionary is not
implemented, and that the STANAG 4609 video container is out of
scope. The encoder accepts any integer key and the decoder returns
raw value bytes, so round-trip is lossy by design. **N7** (decoder
emits hex strings rather than typed values) is the cosmetic surface
of this: the encoder writes `b"foo"` and the decoder yields
`"666f6f"`. Either teach the decoder to attempt UTF-8 with hex
fallback, or update the conformance doc to call out the round-trip
asymmetry explicitly.

### 4.3 OGC SensorThings v1.1 (`src/nous/interop/sensorthings.py`)

`phenomenonTime` and `resultTime` are normalised to UTC ISO-8601 with
the `Z` suffix per OGC SensorThings v1.1 §6.5. The encoder refuses
when the source is older than `max_age_s` (default 60 s); both
encode and decode enforce `max_payload_len` (default 64 KiB). Decode
returns `{"error": ...}` on parse failure rather than raising. Matches
`docs/conformance/ogc-sensorthings.md`.

Documented omissions (streaming via MQTT or websockets, batch
insertion, FeatureOfInterest linking, complex result types) are not
misrepresented as supported.

### 4.4 NMEA 0183 v4.10 (`src/nous/interop/nmea0183.py`)

The encoder emits a fully-specified `$GPGGA` sentence with all
fourteen fields, XOR checksum, ASCII-only enforcement, and explicit
lat / lon range validation. The decoder parses back to the structured
fields, validates the checksum, and rejects non-ASCII payloads. The
encoded string carries the trailing `\r\n` per spec. Matches
`docs/conformance/nmea-0183.md`.

Documented omissions (other sentence types, multi-talker prefixes) are
not misrepresented.

### 4.5 STANAG 4774 / 4778 (`src/nous/interop/stanag_4774.py`)

JSON envelope with `policyIdentifier`, `classification`,
`creationTime` (UTC ISO-8601, `Z`-suffixed), and `payload`. Encoder
refuses when source is older than `max_age_s`; both encode and decode
enforce `max_payload_len`. Decode returns `{"error": ...}` on parse
failure. Matches `docs/conformance/stanag-4774-4778.md`.

The conformance document is explicit that this is a shape-only
adapter and that real STANAG conformance requires a certified
implementation. The code does not pretend otherwise.

### 4.6 MQTT (`src/nous/interop/mqtt.py`)

The adapter exists in code and is discoverable via `interop_formats`,
but there is no `docs/conformance/mqtt.md`. The 2026-05-20 L9 finding
("`interop/mqtt.py` has no `docs/conformance/mqtt.md`") still applies.
A one-page document declaring the QoS policy, retained behaviour,
last-will, and topic ACL would close it.

### 4.7 Frameworks (MOSA, SOSA / SSN, STANAG 4677 DSSS)

`docs/conformance/mosa.md`, `docs/conformance/sosa.md`, and
`docs/conformance/stanag-4677-dsss.md` describe alignment claims
rather than adapters. MOSA is documented as the architectural
posture; SOSA / SSN is documented as the conceptual alignment for
`Observation` and `Estimate` types in `src/nous/types.py`; STANAG
4677 is explicitly documented with "Adapter: None in v0.1". All
three are honest about what they claim and what they do not.

## 5. New observations from the fresh code index

### N8 (Medium). Four Proposed ADRs have no implementation yet

ADR 0019 (deterministic seed and clock seam), ADR 0020 (property-based
invariants for subsystem physics), ADR 0021 (per-subsystem MCP tool
modules), and ADR 0022 (runtime safety enforcer with structured
result) are all in **Proposed** status as of 2026-05-23. The
corresponding seams in the source tree do not exist:

- No `src/nous/safety/enforcer.py`; the FSM guards in
  `src/nous/state/machine.py` continue to enforce SC-2 inline rather
  than through a `SafetyEnforcer.check()` call site.
- No engine-level RNG thread; subsystems that need randomness still
  reach for `numpy.random` globals or per-instance seeds.
- `src/nous/server.py` is still a single ~660-line module rather than
  the per-subsystem split ADR 0021 envisions.
- No `tests/property/` directory; property-based tests are limited to
  `tests/unit/test_estimator_properties.py` and the
  `test_policy_fuzz.py` cases.

This is the intended posture (the ADRs explicitly capture work for a
future PR), but it is worth recording so the Proposed status does not
silently rot. Each ADR is the canonical reference for the work it
gates; the corresponding backlog items should be opened or referenced
explicitly when implementation lands.

### N9 (Low). Two new L1 subsystems have no conformance documents

The sensors and biometrics subsystems landed under PRs #35 and #37 of
the L1 rollout, each with a subsystem implementation, a Kalman
estimator, and an MCP read tool. Neither has an entry under
`docs/conformance/`. The biometrics model card
(`docs/model-cards/estimator-biometrics-kalman.md`) covers the
estimator side, but the subsystem-level interop posture (what an
external operator-state system can read out, what the units imply,
what is parametric versus measured) is undocumented.

This is the same theme as L9 (no MQTT conformance doc): a shipped
adapter or subsystem without a one-page conformance posture. Track
both under the same workstream.

### N10 (Low). `_INSTRUCTIONS` lag is now confirmed at six tools

The `_INSTRUCTIONS` block at `src/nous/server.py:646-661` advertises
13 representative tools. The server registers 19 (the eleven listed
plus `storage_status`, `comms_status`, `position_status`,
`sensors_status`, `biometrics_status`, and `inference_status` from
the L1 rollout). N5 in the 2026-05-23 audit flagged this; the six
under-advertised tools are the L1 subsystem reads. Either extend the
prose block or generate it from the registration loop at server
construction time.

## 7. Recommended remediation order

The 2026-05-23 §10.3 remediation order is still authoritative. This
round's contribution is to add the regression-suite class for each
finding when it closes; the same pattern lands the next closure. The
order, updated for this round, is:

1. **N2 audit sink triage.** Server-side. Still the next live-VM
   action item.
2. **C2 recursive redaction + nested-key test.** Required before
   BL-014 (scenario YAML loader) lands. When the fix lands, add a
   `TestC2RedactionRecurses` class to `tests/regression/test_audit_findings.py`.
3. **H1 runner unit tests.** `tests/unit/test_runner.py` covering
   the four tiers, three policy modes, denial-path `denied=True`
   audit line, exception-to-body mapping, and truncation.
4. **H6 OAuth lock + parent fsync + `chmod 0600` on state files.**
5. **H7 refresh-token family revocation.**
6. **H8 auto-update rollback record + kill-switch documentation
   parity in `SECURITY.md`.**
7. **M1 runner denial `exit_code=1`.**
8. **N3-N7 cleanup.**
9. **M10 profile JSON-schema validation at load time.**
10. **H2 mypy strict for the test tree.**
11. **H9 profile inline placeholder comments.**
12. **L9 / N9 conformance documents** (MQTT, sensors, biometrics).
13. **N8 implementation work** for ADRs 0019 through 0022 (each
    governed by its own ADR; this is a multi-PR programme rather than
    an audit fix).

Items 1 through 7 close the remaining baseline-critical and -high
findings. Items 8 through 12 are opportunistic. Item 13 is design work
that the project has already committed to via the Proposed ADRs.

## 8. Out of scope

Items the 2026-05-20 baseline and the 2026-05-23 audit explicitly
excluded remain out of scope here: stub adapters with `note: lands
with BL-NNN`, mesh / DTN absence, parametric biometrics by design,
absence of a real local model, FSM raising on unknown trigger,
audit.write swallowing internal exceptions. The 2026-05-21 in-depth
review recommendations on assurance depth and EU-sovereignty-oriented
architecture options remain tracked in
[`docs/review-2026-05-21.md`](review-2026-05-21.md) and are not
re-litigated here.

The audit was conducted read-only; no source code changes were made
on the audit branch other than this file, the new ADR 0023, and the
documentation cross-references in the root-level markdown tree.
