# Audit 2026-06-15: adversarial pass after the BL-060 EMCON layer

A fresh adversarial audit at HEAD (`f9ee1ac`, after the three BL-060 EMCON
increments: ADR 0065 emission profiles, ADR 0066 scheduled windows, ADR 0067
metadata minimisation). The pass ran on three parallel fronts: the new EMCON
layer, the comms-denial / store-and-forward / DTN machinery the EMCON work plugs
into, and the cross-cutting integrity invariants (determinism, audit legibility,
tier classification, profile-schema robustness).

The three EMCON findings that are OPSEC-invariant violations introduced by the new
layer (E-1, E-2, E-3 below) are remediated in this same change; the rest are
recorded as BL-098 through BL-108 for their own increments.

## Findings: EMCON layer (remediated in this pass)

### E-1 (HIGH, remediated): a config profile could overwrite a built-in posture

`state/emcon.py` populated the two built-ins (`unrestricted`, `silent`) and then
applied user-defined profiles with `self._profiles[key] = ...` and no guard, so a
`comms.emcon.profiles` entry named `silent` with a non-empty `permit_links`
silently replaced the built-in: an operator who called `emcon_set("silent")` for
radio silence would keep emitting. ADR 0065 makes the two built-ins an invariant
("always present"); the constructor now skips any profile key equal to a built-in,
so the postures are immutable. Pinned by
`test_builtin_postures_cannot_be_overridden_by_config`.

### E-2 (HIGH, remediated): altitude leaked at full precision under a minimising posture

`_POSITION_KEYS` listed only `lat`/`lon`/`latitude`/`longitude`, but the CoT
shaping for `self_model_publish` (`tools/self_model.py`) emits `hae` from the
position estimate's `alt_m`. Under a `minimize` policy with `position_decimals`,
latitude and longitude were coarsened to a grid while altitude passed through at
float64 precision, weakening the OPSEC property the posture claims. The key set now
covers `hae`/`alt`/`alt_m`/`altitude`, and ADR 0067's decision text records the
altitude scope. Pinned by `test_minimize_coarsens_altitude_fields`.

### E-3 (LOW, remediated): an extreme phase could black-hole a window

`_parse_window` accepted any finite `phase_s`, so a value like `1e18` made
`(now_s - phase_s) % period_s` lose period resolution and the window read as
permanently closed, exactly the silent black-hole ADR 0066 says a misconfiguration
must not cause. `phase_s` is now normalised modulo the period at parse time (which
also tidies a negative phase). Pinned by
`test_extreme_phase_s_is_normalised_modulo_period`.
